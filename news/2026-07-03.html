Zhipu AI (Z.ai) released GLM-5.2, an open-weight model with MIT licensing, a 1-million-token context window, and pricing at $1.40/$4.40 per million tokens - roughly one-sixth the cost of GPT-5.5. 3. US Government Nears Voluntary AI Model Release Standards

The US Commerce Department is in advanced talks with AI labs to establish voluntary standards for new model releases, with an announcement possible as soon as next week. The framework would set benchmarks, timelines, and access rules for advanced models. This follows a June executive order directing agencies to test frontier models before public release. The Commerce Department also lifted export controls on Anthropic's Fable and Mythos models this week, less than three weeks after suspending them over national security concerns.

OpenAI has proposed giving the US government a 5% equity stake (valued at approximately $42.6 billion) to ease regulatory pressure, and is urging other AI companies to do the same. 6. Anthropic Explores Custom AI Chip With Samsung

Anthropic is in early talks with Samsung Electronics to manufacture a custom AI chip, joining OpenAI, Google, Amazon, Microsoft, and Meta in the push to reduce dependence on Nvidia. Anthropic has hired chip engineers including Clive Chan, formerly of Tesla and OpenAI's custom chip teams. Samsung offers a 4nm node (already used in Nvidia's LPU 30) and a newer 2nm SF2P process entering production later this year. This follows OpenAI's Broadcom-built "Jalapeno" chip announcement.

7. Nvidia Launches Revenue-Sharing Model for AI Startups

Nvidia introduced a new business model granting token credits to developers in exchange for a share of future sales, aiming to widen access to its AI hardware for startups lacking capital. The company will connect AI data center operators with cloud service providers and earn both standard product revenue and a share of cloud revenue on supported capacity. The move targets researchers and nascent companies that historically couldn't access large-scale AI compute.
